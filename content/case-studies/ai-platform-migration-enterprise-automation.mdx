## Context

NexaServe had multiple disconnected ML and LLM workflows across teams, resulting in duplicate tooling, inconsistent model quality, and weak governance controls.

## Objectives

- Consolidate model training, evaluation, and deployment
- Introduce secure prompt and model governance
- Reduce time-to-production for AI use cases
- Improve reliability of customer-facing AI assistants

## What We Implemented

### Platform Consolidation
Unified experimentation, feature pipelines, and deployment orchestration into a standard MLOps lifecycle.

### AI Governance
Introduced policy checks, prompt/version registry, model evaluation gates, and human-in-the-loop approval for high-risk workflows.

### Production Hardening
Implemented observability for drift, hallucination guardrails, fallback routing, and quality feedback loops.

## Outcomes

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Model Deployment Time | 18 days | 5 days |
| AI Assistant Deflection Rate | 21% | 44% |
| P1 AI Incidents (quarterly) | 11 | 3 |
| Duplicate Tooling Cost | Baseline | -38% |

## Technology Stack

- MLOps orchestration and model registry
- Managed vector search and retrieval augmentation
- Policy-driven AI governance controls
- End-to-end observability for model quality
